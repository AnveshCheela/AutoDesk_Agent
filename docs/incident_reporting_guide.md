## Overview

Every TechCorp Inc. employee has a responsibility to report security incidents promptly. Early detection and reporting are critical to minimizing damage, protecting company data, and maintaining regulatory compliance. **When in doubt, report it.**

## What Constitutes a Security Incident

A security incident is any event that threatens the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of TechCorp's systems or data. Examples include:

- **Phishing or social engineering** — Receiving or falling victim to a deceptive email, call, or message.
- **Malware or ransomware** — Unexpected pop-ups, system slowdowns, or files being encrypted.
- **Unauthorized access** — Someone accessing systems, files, or facilities without proper authorization.
- **Data breach or data leak** — Corporate data exposed to unauthorized parties, whether intentional or accidental.
- **Lost or stolen device** — A company laptop, phone, USB drive, or security badge that is missing.
- **Suspicious account activity** — Logins from unfamiliar locations, password change notifications you didn't initiate, or unexpected MFA prompts.

## How to Report an Incident

Report security incidents **immediately** using one of the following methods:

1. **Email:** Send details to **security@techcorp.com** — monitored 24/7 by the Security Operations Center (SOC).
2. **Phone:** Call the IT Security Hotline at **ext. 4357 (HELP)** — available 24/7 for urgent incidents.
3. **Self-Service Portal:** Submit a ticket at [https://helpdesk.techcorp.com](https://helpdesk.techcorp.com) under **"Security Incident Report."**
4. **In person:** Visit the IT Security office (Building A, Room 142) during business hours.

When reporting, include as much detail as possible: **what happened**, **when it occurred**, **what systems or data are affected**, and **any actions you have already taken**.

## Incident Severity Levels

All reported incidents are triaged and assigned a severity level:

| Severity | Level | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P1** | Critical | Active breach or system-wide impact | Ransomware spreading across the network |
| **P2** | High | Significant threat with potential for escalation | Executive account compromised |
| **P3** | Medium | Contained issue with limited impact | Phishing email reported, no credentials entered |
| **P4** | Low | Minor issue with no immediate risk | Suspicious but benign email received |

## Response Time SLAs

The Information Security team commits to the following response times based on severity:

| Severity | Initial Response | Status Update Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **P1 — Critical** | **15 minutes** | Every 30 minutes until resolved |
| **P2 — High** | **1 hour** | Every 2 hours |
| **P3 — Medium** | **4 hours** | Daily updates |
| **P4 — Low** | **24 hours** | Upon resolution |

## Employee Responsibilities During an Incident

If you suspect or confirm a security incident:

- **Do not attempt to fix it yourself** — report it and let the Security team handle containment.
- **Do not turn off your device** unless explicitly instructed by IT Security (evidence may be needed for forensic analysis).
- **Disconnect from the network** (unplug Ethernet or disable Wi-Fi) if instructed by the Security team.
- **Do not discuss the incident** on public channels or social media. Follow TechCorp's incident communication protocol.
- **Preserve evidence** — do not delete files, emails, or logs related to the incident.
- **Cooperate fully** with the Security and Legal teams during investigation.

## Non-Retaliation Policy

TechCorp Inc. maintains a **strict non-retaliation policy**. Employees who report incidents in good faith will never face disciplinary action, even if the report turns out to be a false alarm. We encourage a culture of proactive reporting.
